USD Cyber Lab, Work and School Converge

Sept 01, 2018

In addition to attending USD as a master’s student I also work on campus full time as a Audio Visual Technician. One of the best benefits of working at USD is the tuition remission program which allows you to take one undergrad or graduate program on the house. The school of Engineering at USD recently received a donation to build out an un used section of the engineering building with labs and classrooms including a Cyber Security Lab. As an AV tech part of my job is to assist with the design and spec of the AV gear that go into all academic spaces on campus. I love this part of my job and working on the Cyber Security Lab was a special treat. The Cyber Lab has the most advanced gear and capabilities of any space on campus. I designed the layout for the control system and specified the necessary programming. The main star of the show is the 16x16 video matrix switcher. It allows you to route any source to any 4 of the 80' displays. The displays are setup pairs, one horizontal one vertical. The idea in mind was to make viewing source code and spec sheets easier. There are seven student pod stations that allow you to hookup a laptop and display it on any of the screens. The lab opens for classes starting in the fall (next week) and I am quite excited to see the space in use. I really enjoyed working on a project where I had some real skin in the game and also opened my eyes to the possibly of pursuing this combination of audio visual and cyber security in a professional capacity
